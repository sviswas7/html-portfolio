Best Shows to Watch on Netflix recommended by Times Of India

Having hundreds of shows available on Netflix that cover a wide range of genres, including comedies, dramas, and historical dramas, choosing what to watch can be difficult. Time of India has put together a list of the most compelling shows that are now accessible on the platform, from Netflix originals to purchased series, to assist you in navigating this vast array of choices. The difficult part now is deciding which to take on first.

TOI's carefully chosen list of the top Netflix series is the perfect place to start if you're looking for your next binge-worthy show for a relaxing night in. Read more

Best TV Shows to Binge Watch on Netflix

Heeramandi

BAFTA-nominated director-composer Sanjay Leela Bhansali's opulent saga Heeramandi chronicles the lives of India's greatest courtesans, or tawaifs, as they struggle for independence from British control in 1920. The courtesans plot to satisfy their hidden cravings and subvert the established order while entertaining aristocrats. Starring in the epic Indian musical series are Aditi Rao Hydari, Sonakshi Sinha, and Manisha Koirala.

The Great Indian Kapil Show

Comedian Kapil Sharma is the host of a hilarious variety talk show that offers viewers lots of entertainment and laughs. The show features celebrity guests, funny antics, and his iconic supporting ensemble.

Dead Boy Detective

Steve Yockey's supernatural horror detective comedy-drama series Dead Boy Detectives is based on characters from Neil Gaiman and Matt Wagner's DC Comics. The characters Charles Rowland and Edwin Paine, played by George Rexstrew, Jayden Revri, Kassius Nelson, Briana Cuoco, Ruth Connell, Yuyu Kitamura, and Jenn Lyon, respectively, decide to stay on Earth after death in order to look into supernatural crimes.

Netflix gave the show the go-ahead in February 2023; it was initially scheduled to debut on HBO Max on April 25, 2024. It takes place in the same universe as The Sandman, with Kirby Howell-Baptiste playing Death once more. Dead Boy Detectives received high marks from critics for its screenplay, directing, performances, and graphics.

Maamla Legal Hai

"Maamla Legal Hai" examines the legal field by presenting a varied cast of solicitors, from young, idealistic solicitors to seasoned pros.

In the show, Kishan plays Patparganj Bar Association President VD Tyagi, who has ambitions to become India's Attorney General. Tyagi is portrayed by Bisht, Grewal, Batra, and Rajoria as dealing with solicitors. The producers of "Maamla Legal Hai" are Khanna, Biswapati Sarkar, Sameer Saxena, and Amit Golani.

Queen of Tears

"Queen of Tears" is a television drama produced in South Korea in 2024, co-directed by Kim Hee-won and Jang Young-woo, and directed by Park Ji-eun. The show, which stars Kim Soo-hyun and Kim Ji-won, tells the tale of a married couple going through a difficult time in their marriage. From March 9, 2024, to April 28, 2024, it broadcast on tvN every Saturday and Sunday at 21:20 (KST). Additionally, the series may be seen on TVING in South Korea and Netflix in several other countries.

With a final episode rating of 24.850% nationwide, the show surpassed "Crash Landing on You" to become the highest-rated tvN series to date. In terms of viewing ratings and total viewers, it also became the third-highest-rated series in the history of Korean cable television.

A Man in Full

Jeff Daniels and Diane Lane star in the limited drama television series "A Man in Full" on American television. Based on Tom Wolfe's book of the same name, it was written and directed by Regina King and Thomas Schlamme. David E. Kelley was the creator. On May 2, 2024, the six-episode series made its Netflix debut.

Baby Reindeer

"Baby Reindeer" is a British miniseries starring Richard Gadd that combines aspects of drama, black humour, and suspense. The series delves into Gadd's actual experience of being sexually abused and stalked throughout his twenties, and is based on his autobiographical one-man play of the same name. Gadd is paired with Jessica Gunning and Nava Mau in this film, which was directed by Weronika Tofilska and Josephine Bornebusch.

All seven episodes of the series premiered simultaneously on Netflix on April 11, 2024. "Baby Reindeer" garnered critical acclaim and topped Netflix's Global Weekly Top 10 TV (English) list.

The Asunta Case

Spanish crime thriller miniseries "The Asunta Case" (Spanish: El caso Asunta) debuted in 2024. Jon de la Cuesta, David Orea Arribas, Gema R. Neira, and Ramón Campos designed it. Candela Peña and Tristán Ulloa appear in the serial, which is based on the murder of Asunta Basterra.

The plot centres on the investigation into the Asunta Basterra case, which began on September 21, 2013, when her parents, Rosario Porto and Alfonso Basterra, filed a missing person's report. Her body was later found on a road close to Santiago de Compostela.

Avatar: The Last Airbender

Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko's well-known animated series "Avatar: The Last Airbender" has been adapted for Netflix. The live-action adaption, which was created by Albert Kim, stars Gordon Cormier, Kiawentiio, Ian Ousley, Dallas Liu, Paul Sun-Hyung Lee, Ken Leung, and Daniel Dae Kim among its ensemble cast.

Water Tribes, Earth Kingdom, Fire Nation, and Air Nomads are the four nations that symbolise the classical elements in the imaginary universe where the show is situated. In every country, there are specific people known as "benders" who have the ability to control one of these factors. The protagonist of the tale is twelve-year-old Aang, the only Air Nomad to survive and the only Avatar capable of mastering all four elements.

Monk

The USA Network ran the well-liked American detective TV show "Monk" from 2002 until 2009. Tony Shalhoub plays Adrian Monk, a private investigator suffering from obsessive-compulsive disorder, in the television series. While looking into the murder of his wife, he collaborates with the San Francisco Police Department to solve peculiar cases. The show delves into the characters' inner lives and struggles.

ABC originally intended "Monk" to be a police drama, but in 1998, they added an OCD-afflicted investigator, and the programme took on a new and distinctive shape. After Tony Shalhoub was cast and the series was picked up by the USA Network, it became popular. Los Angeles, Toronto, and Vancouver served as filming locations.
